    AGE Methodology · Version 1.0

    AI Governance Evidence (AGE) Methodology

    AGE is a practical governance methodology for evaluating publicly available AI governance evidence.

    Core principle

    Evaluate what is disclosed, not what is merely implied.

    AGE does not determine whether an AI system is fair, unfair, compliant, or noncompliant. AGE helps reviewers evaluate the quality, scope, transparency, and completeness of the governance evidence available in the documentation provided.

    01

    Evidence over assumption

    Findings are limited to what the provided text explicitly supports.

    02

    Absence is not proof

    “Not found in the provided text” means additional documentation may be needed.

    03

    Documentation, not verdicts

    Scores assess documentation completeness and do not make legal or performance conclusions.

    i

    Evidence boundary: AGE evaluates only the text provided. An absent disclosure should be treated as “not found in the provided text,” not as proof that the evidence does not exist.

    About AGE

    An evolving governance methodology

    The AI Governance Evidence (AGE) Methodology is an evolving governance methodology developed by AQ'S Corner LLC. It is designed to help reviewers evaluate publicly available AI governance evidence using a structured, evidence-based review process. The methodology will continue to evolve as additional governance artifacts and real-world documentation are evaluated.
